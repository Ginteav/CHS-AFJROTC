Choctawhatchee High School AFJROTC Unit History

Established in 1967, Choctawhatchee High School’s Air Force Junior ROTC program (unit designation FL-022) has been a proud part of the Fort Walton Beach community for close to 60 years. Since its founding, the program has focused on developing cadets into leaders of character, emphasizing citizenship, service, and aerospace education.

From its early days with a small group of pioneering cadets, Choctawhatchee’s AFJROTC has steadily grown into a distinguished program known for academic excellence, leadership development, and thousands of hours of community service each year. Over the decades, the unit has expanded its curriculum to include aerospace science, leadership education, STEM enrichment, and dual-enrollment opportunities with Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University.

Today, FL-022 continues to thrive—preparing the next generation of leaders for careers in military service, aviation, and beyond. The legacy of "Big Green" pride and service remains strong as the program continues to evolve, inspire, and achieve.
